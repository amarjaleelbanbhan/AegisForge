channel so nothing gets lost.

## Before you ask

A little homework saves everyone time:

1. **Search first** — check [existing issues](https://github.com/amarjaleelbanbhan/CortexWard/issues)
   and [Discussions](https://github.com/amarjaleelbanbhan/CortexWard/discussions); your question may
   already be answered.
2. **Read the docs** — the [README](README.md) covers install and usage, [ARCHITECTURE.md](ARCHITECTURE.md)
   explains the design, and [ROADMAP.md](ROADMAP.md) shows what's built vs. planned.
3. **Include context** — version/commit, OS, Python version, the exact command you ran, and the full
   output. A reproducible example gets a much faster answer.

## Response expectations

CortexWard is an early-stage, community-driven open-source project maintained on a best-effort
basis. There is **no commercial support or SLA**. Issues and discussions are triaged as time allows;
clear, reproducible reports are prioritized. The fastest way to get something fixed is often to
[open a pull request](CONTRIBUTING.md).
